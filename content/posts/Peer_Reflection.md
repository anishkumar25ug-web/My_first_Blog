Peer Reflection on Algorithmic Bias
Initially when I used AI applications or tools like ChatGPT, Cloud and so on, I used to wonder about the functionality and key features of the system. At the same time I also wonder about the all correct possible answers asked by the users. I also had a core question about how these algorithms and systems work. From today’s presentation on Algorithmic Bias, my thoughts and questions were resonating and forced me to think and understand the topic more thoroughly. I got to know that these tools work on the data which is already stored in the  big database. 
Through the discussion there was an important aspect of these AIs that is only reflecting on the information that is getting fed to them, some of the AI has the immense capability to generate new aspects of those data but ultimately it relies on stored data. Just think of a country like India which has a lot of diversity in language, religion, culture, caste etc. But these AIs are only trained on specific types of data and give answers accordingly. But it can raise a curious problem- it can give unfair answers/results. Also it can influence our decision by providing one aspect of information. (I understood this during the Q&A). It's not the mistake of these AIs or we also can’t blame the developers, because those developer thinking abilities developed like that by living in such a society. They are just trying to implement. This information gives me a broader aspect of looking at these such systems, now I know it also can be wrong. I know it can cover most of the things but not completely, Before leading to any conclusion or important decision now I will also think about these aspects.
